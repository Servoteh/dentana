A gentle approach for our youngest

Pediatric Dentistry

The goal of the first visit is not to “fix everything at once,” but for the child to build trust. When the first experience is good, every next visit is easier.

Our approach with children

We approach the child gradually and without pressure, explaining each step in a way they understand and never forcing anything. That way the office becomes a safe place rather than a source of stress.

Prevention for children

The best protection is regular check-ups, proper hygiene and, when indicated, protective measures. We give parents clear advice for caring for the teeth at home.

FAQ

Pediatric dentistry — questions and answers

My child is afraid of the dentist — how do you handle that?

Patiently and without force. The first visit is short and relaxed, so the child gets used to the office and the team before any treatment. We adapt the pace to the child.

At what age should I bring my child for a check-up?

We recommend the first short check-up visits from an early age — before a problem appears, so the child builds trust.

Do baby teeth need to be treated?

Yes. Baby teeth matter for chewing, speech and the proper development of the permanent teeth, so they are treated and preserved.

Does treatment hurt?

We work gently and, when needed, under local anesthesia, so the child stays calm and pain-free.
